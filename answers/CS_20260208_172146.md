## Direct Answer
The primary difference between Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) and Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs) lies in their architecture and the type of data they are designed to process. CNNs are particularly suited for image and video processing, using convolutional layers to extract features from spatial data. In contrast, RNNs are designed for sequential data such as time series data, speech, or text, using recurrent connections to capture temporal relationships.

## Verified Facts
* **CNNs**: 
  * Utilize convolutional and pooling layers to extract spatial features.
  * Excel in image and video classification, object detection, and segmentation tasks.
  * Can be used for other types of data with spatial hierarchies.
* **RNNs**:
  * Employ recurrent layers to process sequential data.
  * Are suited for tasks involving temporal dependencies such as language modeling, speech recognition, and time series forecasting.
  * Can suffer from vanishing or exploding gradients, which are mitigated by variants like LSTMs and GRUs.

## Learning Resources
For a deeper understanding, exploring resources on deep learning frameworks like TensorFlow or PyTorch, which provide implementations of both CNNs and RNNs, can be beneficial. Additionally, academic papers and online courses on neural networks offer comprehensive insights into their design and application.

## Summary
In summary, CNNs and RNNs are both powerful tools in the field of deep learning, each with its own strengths tailored to different types of data. Understanding their differences is crucial for selecting the appropriate model for a given task.

For practical application, it's recommended to start with simple projects using pre-trained models and then move on to designing and training your own models as you gain more experience. Leveraging libraries and frameworks can significantly simplify the development process.
